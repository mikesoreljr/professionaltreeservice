Market Analysis

  • Industry Overview: Bill $800-1,000 per hour all inclusive debris removal fees. Wood dumps are not as far as the regular dump, seperate dump. Chippers rent for $450 a day. Many competent workers & current businesses willing to subcontract. New technologies like tracked bucket lifts can fit thru gates and go up to 60-70 foot so making quick work of large trees has never been easier.
  • Target Market: Detailed description of your ideal customers, including demographics and buying behavior. Homeowners within the Markham Woods Road Corridor, Sweetwater, Apopka, & Windermere/Dr. Phillips, even Mount Dora/Sorrento areas. Ideal customers live in non-gated communities with no HOA's. Houses with mature trees, big yards to fit machinery within.
  • Market Needs: They need licenses & insurances, a superior service for a fair price, talented workers, professional appearances. We support local barbecue businesses by supplying them with firewood.
  • Market Trends: Ai, will remove many office labor jobs, so the demand for tree work will remain. Robots in 10-20 years will be able to climb and remove trees. Grapple saws on the ends of Knucklebooms function as robotic arms currently. More knucklebooms and bucket lifts will exist making takedowns much safer with high tech equipment. There are more companies recycling the wood turning it into wood pulp, and people are requesting chip drops (free chips) to use in their yard instead of costly mulch.
  • Competitive Analysis: My 2 competitors are my associates, they charge up to $1,000 per hour and these prices are market rate. There are cheaper companies that offer a substandard service and undercut the professionals. There are plenty of trees in Seminole, Lake & Orange Counties.
